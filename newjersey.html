The New Jersey Coastline (cont'd)

There are some noteworthy sights along the New Jersey coastline.

Jersey City:

The most prominent area on the Jersey coastline is Jersey City, which presents an impressive skyline of modern skyscrapers. These building mostly date from the post 9/11 period, when Financial District firms were looking for an inexpensive, secure and nearby location for back offices.

Port Newark and Elizabeth Marine Terminal:

The largest container port on the East Coast. It is also home to IKEA and is served by the New Jersey Turnpike.

The Bayonne Bridge:

Spanning the Kill Van Kull, the body of water that separates New Jersey from Staten Island, this bridge connects port facilities in the two states.
