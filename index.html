HARBOR ISLANDS

There are three major islands in the harbor, the largest and perhaps least known being Governors Island. The other two are the historic Ellis Island and Liberty Island which hosts the most famous monument in the world, the Statue of Liberty. ... more.

THE MANHATTAN SKYLINE

The most iconic skyline in the world is the view of Manhattan Island from New York Harbor. For several decades it was dominated by the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center. Now the new WTC Tower takes that place of honor. ... more.

SOUTH BROOKLYN

From Brooklyn Heights all the way to the Verrazzano Bridge, the South Brooklyn shoreline defines the eastern borders of the harbor. After the Heights come Red Hook, Gowanus, Sunset Park and Bay Ridge. And in the background, the modest skyline of Downtown Brooklyn. ... more.

STATEN ISLAND

To the south is the North Shore of Staten Island, the least populated of New York City's five boroughs. ... more.

NEW JERSEY COASTLINE

To the west is New Jersey and beyond that, the continental United States. Across from the Financial District is the modern skyline of Jersey City, then the historic city of Hoboken. After that comes a mysterious collection of docks, tanker farms and working class enclaves, all the way down to the Bayonne Bridge. ... more.
