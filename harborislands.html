Harbor Islands (cont'd)

Liberty Island:

Home to the Statue of Liberty. The Statue was created in Paris, France. It was designed by Frederic Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustav Eiffel.

Governors Island:

Governors Island was fortified during the War of 1812 to protect Manhattan from the British. It served as a Coast Guard facility for many years and was turned over to the City of New York in 1996. In 2001 it became part of the National Park Service.

Ellis Island:

This island was the first landing spot for millions of immigrants to the US during the period of 1892 - 1954. The iconic main building was built in 1900.
